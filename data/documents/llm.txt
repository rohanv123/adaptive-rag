Large Language Models (LLMs) are artificial intelligence systems trained on vast amounts of text data. They can generate human-like responses, answer questions, summarize content, and perform various language tasks.

LLMs use deep learning techniques, especially transformer architectures, to understand and generate text. However, they have limitations such as hallucination, where they may produce incorrect or misleading information.

To improve reliability, LLMs are often combined with retrieval systems like RAG. This allows them to access external knowledge sources and provide more accurate and up-to-date answers.
